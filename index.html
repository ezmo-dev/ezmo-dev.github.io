Overview

After-party for the Adopting Bitcoin conference held at Edificio Morazan in San Salvador. Over 635 attendees experienced a fully Lightning-powered event featuring interactive hardware installations that accepted Bitcoin payments in real time.

8 local artists participated. Lightning-powered catering generated over $5,000 in revenue. More than 200 Blink wallets were created on-site, and 120+ NFC cards were distributed.

Overview

Plan ₿ Forum at the Marte Museum in San Salvador. Deployed at the request of Plan ₿ Forum to provide multiple Lightning-activated installations across the venue. Building on lessons from the Adopting Bitcoin after party with new and upgraded devices.

New installations included AI-powered generative visuals triggered by Lightning payments, displayed on a large LED panel on the main stage. Two giant lasers were positioned at the base of a large statue inside the museum, powerful enough to be seen from outside the venue. The lasers were also synchronized during a drone show. In collaboration with a Salvadorian company, a VJing setup projected Bitcoin-themed video montages on a large wall of the museum.

Hardware

Smoke Machine — Upgraded iteration of the LNBits Instance + BitcoinSwitchFirmware + ESP32 & relay system.

Prompt to VJing — TouchDesigner + StreamDiffusion + DMX controller. A Lightning payment sends a text prompt that generates real-time AI visuals projected at the event.

Treasure Hunt — Lightning-activated lock: finding the 12 hidden QR codes around the venue and paying the last QR code on a treasure box unlocks a physical locker and wins the prizes.

Laser DMX Controller — Laser lighting rig triggered and controlled via Lightning payments.

Lightning Treasure Chest Smoke Machine
Read case study
Overview

First European deployment at BTC Prague. The flagship installation was a Pirate-themed Lightning Treasure Chest — a physical puzzle box that emits smoke and releases a mechanical lock upon Lightning payment. The smoke machine was displayed in a transparent case with exposed wiring as an educational exhibit.

Smoke machine booth generated approximately $80 in revenue over the event days.

Treasure Hunt Format

12 seed words hidden across the venue and at partner booths (with Bitcoin trivia challenges). Words were distributed in sealed envelopes. The final QR code on the chest — once scanned and paid — triggered the ESP32: smoke machine fires, then the electric locker releases.

Collaborated with RockstarDev: 6 treasure chest clones were distributed, but only 3 carried the real password — adding a layer of deduction to the hunt.

Hardware

Lightning Treasure Chest (Pirate theme) — ESP32, 12V battery, 2 relays: one for the electric locker, one for the smoke machine remote.

Smoke Machine — Transparent case with exposed wiring, displayed as an educational booth installation.

Read case study
Overview

A week-long educational residency at PoW.Space in Lugano for 21 students from around the world. The curriculum integrated Lightning hardware deployments with live lectures, hands-on building sessions, and a multi-day gamified treasure hunt.

Guest speakers included Preston Pysh and Efrat Fenigson. Key contributors: Kukks (BTCPay Server), ARK Labs, and Boltz.

Technical Stack Evolution

Started with BTCPay Server + Blink node. Under classroom load, Blink node response times caused 10-minute latency spikes — root cause identified as Blink node slowness, not BTCPay. Migrated mid-week to LNbits.

Final production stack: self-custodial Ark wallet + Boltz (Lightning interoperability) + BTCPay Server node.

BTCPay Server Plugin

Released a new open-source BTCPay Server plugin during the school: GPIO activation / hardware control through payments. Enables direct physical hardware triggering from BTCPay Server payment events.

Treasure Hunt Format

Daily reset at midday. Each day's questions were based on the previous day's lecture. 12 keywords collected over the week to form a final passphrase. Decoding challenges included Caesar ciphers, ROT13/21, and SHA-256 hash puzzles.

Overview

Baltic Honeybadger in Riga — one of the largest deployments to date, spanning the Chill Zone, Workshop Stage, and an after party at Teritorija (an old abandoned factory). The bar at the after party ran entirely on Ark-powered Bitcoin payments.

Workshop: "IoT Powered by Lightning" — a live breakdown of the smoke machine from hardware to software, on the Workshop Stage.

After party performers: Rockamoto, Rolling Dynamite, Mark Kadek, and a fire show. Treasure hunt: 2 winners.

Hardware

Giant Honeybadger Head — Custom large-format prop that emits smoke on Lightning payment. Signature installation for the event.

Smoke Machine — Standard deployment, booth at the conference.

Flying Photo Booth — Takes and distributes photos triggered by Lightning payment.

Treasure Hunt Chest — Lightning-activated treasure chest with multi-location puzzle.

Ark-Powered Bar — The entire after-party bar accepted Bitcoin payments via Ark, with no Lightning channel management required.
